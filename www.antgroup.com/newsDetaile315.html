Alibaba Finance Arm Follows Jack Ma in Overseas Expansion

2014-10-16
The payment-processing affiliate of Alibaba Group Holding Ltd. is expanding in the U.S. and Russia as billionaire founder Jack Ma pushes the Chinese e-commerce giant beyond its home market.

Zhejiang Ant Small & Micro Financial Services Group Co. is reorganizing itself into six business units, including the Paypal-like Alipay, as it aims to process the digital sales of more small businesses around the world, company officials said at a conference in Beijing yesterday. The units are focused on areas where they already have a presence, such as Brazil and Spain, as well as rural China, where Alipay hopes to reach 100 million users in as soon as two years.

The expansion follows Alibaba Group Holding Ltd.’s record $25 billion initial public offering last month, which made the Hangzhou-based e-commerce company more valuable than Amazon.com and EBay Inc. combined. Small & Micro Financial, which has no ties to Alibaba other than Ma, is rebranding itself Ant Financial Services Group as it considers a possible share sale later.

“When Alibaba Group listed, one of the three main strategic goals was going global,” said Sabrina Peng, the financial company’s vice president for international business. “Ant Financial will work closely with Alibaba Group. We want to help small businesses expand globally just like large corporations can, and we want to make it less difficult for Chinese consumers to buy international brands.”

Alipay Expansion

Small & Micro Financial, controlled by Ma, is competing against Tencent Holdings Ltd. to break into China’s estimated $27 trillion banking industry with payment services and wealth management products. In addition to Alipay, its services include the money market fund Yu’E Bao, investment platform Zhao Cai Bao, small loans business Ant Credit, banking operation MYbank and the Alipay Wallet application.

Alipay has 17.9 million active users overseas in more than 100 countries and is accepted by 2,000 merchants overseas, Peng said. The service has seen slightly faster growth in third- and fourth-tier cities than in larger ones.

The mobile version Alipay Wallet handles 45 million transactions a day and has 190 million active users, said Liu Lejun, general manager of the mobile business unit. Alipay Wallet products allow users to pay for hospital fees and rent cars in Beijing and Hangzhou, according to a display at the conference.

IPO Valuation

Alibaba in 2011 transferred Alipay to Small & Micro Financial -- which is controlled by Ma -- amid concern that it wouldn’t be permitted to conduct business in China while it has foreign ownership, the IPO filing shows. That sale was criticized by shareholder Yahoo! Inc., which said it wasn’t informed of the sale at the time, and led to an agreement on compensation.

Alibaba indicated in an August filing with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission that its financial affiliate may conduct an IPO in the future, provided it could reach an implied equity value of at least $25 billion. Alibaba will get at least $9.4 billion from the future value of its financial affiliate and take a one-third stake in the company, according to the filing.

Eric Jing, Small & Micro Financial’s chief financial officer, said yesterday that the company will sell about 20 percent of its shares to strategic investors with 40 percent to go to executives and managers.
